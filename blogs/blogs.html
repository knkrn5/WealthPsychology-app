IDFC First Bank Analysis: -

1.On the basis of Asset Quality (GNPA and NNPA)- Gross Non-Performing Assets (GNPA):

Definition: The total amount of loans that are not being repaid by borrowers.

Importance: Indicates the overall health of the bankâ€™s loan portfolio. A lower GNPA ratio suggests better credit management.

Industry Benchmark: For private banks in India, the average GNPA ratio was 4.5% as of March 31, 2023.

IDFC FIRST Bank Status: Has a GNPA ratio around 2%, indicating good asset quality.

Net Non-Performing Assets (NNPA):

Definition: The amount of GNPA after deducting provisions made by the bank.

Importance: Reflects the actual risk posed by bad loans after accounting for potential losses.

Industry Benchmark: The average NNPA ratio for private banks was 2.5%.

IDFC FIRST Bank Status: Around 1%, showing effective management of bad loans.

2. On the basis of potential losses from NPA- Provision Coverage Ratio (PCR)

Definition: Provisions made against NPAs to cover expected losses from NPAs.

Formula:Provisions = GNPA - NNPA and PCR = Total Provisions / Total NPAs.

Importance: A higher PCR indicates a strong buffer against bad loans. RBI mandates a minimum PCR of 70%.

IDFC FIRST Bank Status: Increasing PCR is a positive sign, showing the bank is prepared for potential defaults and can cover losses without significantly impacting profits

3.On the basis of Deposits- CASA Ratio:

Definition: The ratio of current and savings account deposits to total deposits.

Importance: Indicates the stability of the bankâ€™s deposit base. Higher CASA ratios mean more stable and lower-cost funding.

Industry Benchmark: The average CASA ratio for the industry is around 38%.

4. On the basis of Profitability-Net Interest Margin (NIM):

Definition: The difference between the interest income generated and the interest paid out relative to the bank's interest-earning assets.

Formula: NIM = (Interest Income - Interest Expense) / Average Earning Assets.

Importance: Measures the efficiency of the bankâ€™s investment decisions and its ability to manage its interest spread.

IDFC FIRST Bank Status: Not directly mentioned in the document, but it's a critical ratio for profitability analysis.

5. On the basis of Liquidity of Banks- Capital Adequacy Ratio(CAR):

Definition: A measure of a bank's capital, expressed as a percentage of its risk-weighted assets.

Formula: CAR = (Tier 1 Capital + Tier 2 Capital) / Risk-Weighted Assets.

Importance: Ensures the bank has enough capital to absorb potential losses and protect depositors.

Regulatory Requirement: RBI mandates a minimum CAR of 9%.

IDFC FIRST Bank Status: Not specified in the document, but crucial for assessing financial stability.
